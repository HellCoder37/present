Paw Pets Foundation, Non-Profit Organization

IDA INDIA is a non profit grass root level animal protection organisation, dedicated towards establishing and defending the rights of all non - human living creatures.

It aims at:

  • Providing infrastructural facilities for shelters, dispensaries, ambulances etc for alleviating the suffering of animals.
  • Lobbying for amendments to existing animal protection laws in India, to make them effective.
  • Rescue of abused animals.
  • Campaigning to secure legal recognition for animal rights nationally and internationally.
  • Promoting vegetarianism.
  • Promoting Animal Birth Control program and discouraging brutal extermination of stray animals.
  • Creating awareness amongst people, so as to start a mass movement against -
    • Use of animals in laboratories for experimentation.
    • Vivi-section in schools & colleges
    • Harsh training regimes in circuses and films
    • Manhandling of slaughter animal and poultry chicken
    • Cruelty to cows, bullocks, horses, donkeys,
    • Zoos
    • Breeding for commercial exploitation.

Naazneen Katrak

Founder of the NGO Paw Pets Foundation,
Non-Profit Organization

Actively running a socks manufacturing unit in Palghar A self appointed keeper of all animals both big and small within the available personal finances that could be deployed for rescuing, feeding, sheltering since many years and lately neutering of strays. Since the dog population has grown exponentially feeding and neutering expenses too have increased tremendously. In order to carry out the above objectives and give it a formal recognition, the three partners in the company decided on incorporating the Pet Paws Foundation through which they can fulfill humanitarian ways and means to preserve and give dignity to the animal kingdom. Most important vaccinating the maximum number of strays from the stage of newborn puppies till they mature enough to be neutered. Anti-rabies vaccination being the most important especially for human protection. Another very important aspect is to give dignified last rites to all strays by organising a crematorium for them in Palghar. None such is at present available and normally the bodies of dogs and cats and even cows and buffalos , hit and killed by vehicles are dumped into garbage boxes along the way. If a crematorium is available and run efficiently at a no cost expense, this undignified unhygienic way of disposal can be eliminated. There is a vital requirement for an Ambulance and hospital for the injured and neutering process. Once the injuries are healed, we need to nurture them back to health before shifting them to their original location. media support will be essential to find good homes for adoption of deserted animals as well. At present these are goals we have set and hope to achieve results as soon as possible with people of similar mindset. One may not love animals , but let compassion take over the silent species.
